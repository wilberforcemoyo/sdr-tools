The SDR Operating System —
what it is and who built it

Most outbound operations are built around people, not systems. The SDR Operating System was built to fix that — permanently.

What is the SDR Operating System

The SDR Operating System is a five-pillar diagnostic framework that installs the operating model your SDR team runs on — regardless of who is in the seat. It is not a playbook. It is not a training programme. It is an operating system — designed to run whether you are in the room or not.

Each pillar addresses a specific failure mode in outbound operations: how accounts are targeted, how they are filtered before entering sequences, how reps execute against a standard, how the week is diagnosed before problems compound, and how four leading signals are monitored to catch variance early.

When all five pillars are installed, the team produces consistent pipeline. When one is missing, the system shows exactly which one — and what it is costing.

The five pillars
01
The Targeting Engine
ICP definition, account sourcing, ABM intent layer, tech stack readiness, rep onboarding standard. The foundation before sequences run.
02
The Gatekeeper
Two verified filters every account clears before entering a sequence — Problem Likelihood and Role Accessibility. No manual override.
03
The Consistency Layer
Same steps, every rep, every time. The execution standard reps are scored against weekly. When multiple reps fail the same step — the step is broken, not the rep.
04
The Diagnostic Cadence
A five-day diagnostic rhythm. Monday through Friday. Problems caught at the start of the week — not at the Friday forecast.
05
The Signal Tower
Four leading indicators reviewed weekly — Re-Entry Rate, Abandonment Rate, Composition Score, Meeting-to-Opportunity Rate. The early warning layer that catches variance before it reaches the forecast.
