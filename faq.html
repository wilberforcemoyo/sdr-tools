Questions about the SDR OS?

Most common questions from prospects thinking about whether an SDR OS audit or installation is right for their team.

ABOUT THE SYSTEM
What exactly is the SDR OS?
The SDR Operating System is a diagnostic framework based on five pillars: Targeting Engine, Gatekeeper, Consistency Layer, Diagnostic Cadence, and Signal Tower. But it also includes two foundation layers: how you hire SDRs and how you pay them. It's designed to work whether you're in the room or not. Most teams treat SDR management as hiring good people and hoping. The OS is the system those people run on.
Is this a playbook or a software tool?
Neither. It's a system architecture. You own it completely. We help you design it, install it in your tools (HubSpot, Salesforce, etc.), and train your team to operate it. After the installation, it's yours to maintain and evolve.
Why is it focused on hiring and compensation?
Because most SDR systems fail at the people layer. You can't install a quality-focused system on people hired for activity. You can't maintain a sustainable rhythm with people who are burning out. Fixing the pillars without fixing hiring and compensation is like building a house on sand. We've learned this the hard way across three eras of SDR management.
What's the difference between the five pillars and the hiring/compensation layers?
Hiring and compensation are the *foundation*. If those are broken, the pillars fail. The five pillars are the *operating model*. Together, they create a complete system. We always address the foundation first, then build the pillars.
ABOUT THE AUDIT
What's the difference between the free diagnostic and the paid audit?
The free diagnostic is a self-guided tool: 20 minutes, you answer 20 questions, you get scores on all five pillars. The paid audit ($4.5K-$12K) is us running the diagnostic *with* you, then providing a detailed 8-10 page report that includes: a priority sequence for which pillars to fix first, estimated financial impact of each fix, and a conversation to clarify next steps.
How long does the paid audit take?
Start to finish: about 5 business days. We collect information (1-2 days), score all five pillars (1 day), write the report (1-2 days), deliver it to your inbox. You typically have a 20-minute call after to walk through findings.
Does the audit tell us if we should do a full installation?
Yes. The audit scores each pillar and identifies which ones are broken. Some teams need a full installation. Some teams just need 1-2 pillars fixed. The audit report tells you which is which — and what the cost/benefit is for each approach.
Who needs to be involved in the audit?
Ideally: VP of Sales, RevOps (if you have one), and one manager/team lead. We'll interview them separately. The more perspective we have, the better the diagnosis.
ABOUT INSTALLATION
How long is a full installation?
Eight weeks. This includes: hiring profile redesign, compensation model redesign, all five pillars built and installed in your tools, team training, and 30 days of monitoring. It's not a passive consulting engagement. We're building the system with your team.
Can we do a partial installation (just fix one or two pillars)?
Absolutely. Some teams just need the Targeting Engine fixed. Others need Consistency Layer + Diagnostic Cadence. The audit tells you which pillars are most broken, and we can scope an engagement around just those. Partial installations are typically 4-6 weeks.
Do you work with our existing CRM or do we need to switch?
We work with what you have. HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive — we can build the system in any of them. If you're evaluating CRM choices, we can advise, but we're not dependent on a specific tool.
What happens after the 8 weeks?
You own the system completely. We provide a 30-day handoff period where we're on call for questions. After that, it's yours to maintain. Some teams bring us back quarterly to optimize, but that's optional.
What if we only have 3-4 SDRs? Does SDR OS scale that small?
Yes. The framework doesn't require a minimum size. A 3-person team still benefits from: hiring the right type of person, paying for quality, having a diagnostic rhythm, and early signal detection. Some concepts (like detailed consistency layers) might look different at smaller scale, but the core principles apply.
What if we're an enterprise sales team, not SMB?
The OS scales to enterprise. The pillars are still relevant, but the metrics change. Instead of "meeting-to-opportunity," you track "account engagement and buying committee mapping." Instead of "30-day meetings," you track "early meetings with decision makers." We've worked with both enterprise and SMB teams — the architecture is the same, the KPIs are different.
ABOUT RESULTS & ROI
How long before we see results?
Early wins: 2-3 weeks (hiring profile changes, comp shifts, early signal detection). Meaningful changes: 6-8 weeks (full system running, baseline metrics established). Major impact: 3-6 months (market data shows true pipeline improvement, retention metrics stabilize).
What's the typical ROI?
It varies, but we typically see: 40-60% reduction in SDR activity while maintaining or improving output (fewer meetings, higher quality), 50-80% improvement in meeting-to-opportunity conversion, 40-60% reduction in turnover, and 20-40% reduction in cost per qualified opportunity. The ROI calculation depends on your current state, but most clients recover the installation cost within 6-8 weeks.
What if we don't see results?
We track metrics throughout the installation. If things aren't moving by week 4-5, we diagnose why and adjust the approach. Sometimes it's a tool issue, sometimes it's a process issue, sometimes it's a team adoption issue. We don't just hand off a system — we stay involved until it's working.
Are results guaranteed?
Not in writing, but our approach is systematic. The audit tells you if SDR OS is a fit for your team. If it is, and you commit to running the system, results follow. We've seen it across dozens of teams in different industries. But we also know when it's not the right approach — sometimes the problem is product-market fit, not system. An honest audit will tell you.
LOGISTICS & PROCESS
How much time do we need to commit?
From your team: 5-10 hours per week during the 8-week installation. VP of Sales is more involved early (scoping), less involved late. Managers are involved throughout (training teams). Individual SDRs: 2-3 hours for onboarding, then just running the system daily.
Can this happen while we're still operating normally?
Yes. We build the system parallel to your current operation, then transition. There's a 1-2 week period where both systems run simultaneously, then you flip over to the new OS. It's minimally disruptive.
What if there's leadership turnover during the installation?
It slows things down, but doesn't stop them. We can brief new leaders quickly. The key is that the *system* survives, not the person. If the OS is designed well, a leadership change shouldn't crater everything.
Can we do this remotely?
Yes. All work is done remotely. We may do 1-2 in-person days if you're a large enterprise, but it's not required.
What's the next step after I run the free diagnostic?
