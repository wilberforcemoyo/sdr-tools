General
What exactly is the SDR Operating System? +
It is a two-layer framework that redesigns your entire SDR function. Layer 1 is the People Foundation — who you hire and how you pay them. Layer 2 is the Operating System — five diagnostic pillars that produce consistent, predictable pipeline. Together they make your outbound motion run independently of any individual rep or manager.
How is this different from hiring an SDR consultant? +
A consultant gives you advice and leaves. The SDR Operating System installs infrastructure that runs after the engagement ends. Your team owns the diagnostic cadence, the signal tracking, the execution standard, and the hiring profile permanently. The goal is to make yourself unnecessary — not to create dependency.
We already have a playbook. Why do we need this? +
A playbook tells reps what to do. The SDR Operating System tells you whether they are doing it, whether it is working, and why it is or is not. Most playbooks describe the system — they do not run it. The difference is measurable within four weeks.
Do you work with teams outside the UK? +
Yes. Wilberforce has 18 years of experience across the US, UK, and South Africa. All engagements run remotely. Time zones in the US, UK, and South Africa are all covered.
Fit & Eligibility
What size team does the SDR OS work best for? +
3 to 30 SDRs is the optimal range. Small enough to install the system quickly, large enough to see measurable variance across the team. Teams of 1 to 2 SDRs can still benefit but the ROI is proportionally smaller. Teams above 30 typically need a phased approach — contact us to discuss.
We are pre-Series A. Is this right for us? +
Only if you have product-market fit established and at least 3 SDRs running outbound sequences. If you are still finding your ICP or your ACV is not yet stable, the SDR OS will surface correct problems but the inputs will keep changing. Fix product-market fit first, then install the system.
Our pipeline is mostly inbound. Can we still use the SDR OS? +
The SDR OS is designed for outbound and mixed-motion teams. If 80% or more of your pipeline is inbound, the framework is not the right fit. If you have any meaningful outbound component — even 20% — the diagnostic tools will surface what is broken in it.
We tried a similar system before and it did not stick. What makes this different? +
Most SDR systems fail because they are installed on top of broken people architecture. If you hire volume grinders and pay for meetings booked, any execution standard fights the incentive structure every week. The SDR OS starts with Layer 1 — hiring and compensation — before installing the five pillars. That is why it sticks.
The Audit
What does the free audit actually show me? +
The Full Audit scores all five pillars across your operation. You see exactly which pillar is lowest, what the financial cost of that gap is monthly, and which pillar to fix first. It generates a printable report for your VP Sales or CRO. It takes 20 minutes and requires no sign-up for the first four pillars — Pillar 4 unlocks with your email.
Is the free audit really free? What is the catch? +
It is genuinely free. No credit card. The email gate at Pillar 4 sends you three resources: the HubSpot signal setup guide, the ABM integration guide, and your audit PDF. That is the exchange — your email for three documents. If your audit score reveals a clear gap, you will have enough context to decide whether to book a diagnostic call.
How accurate is the audit? +
The audit is as accurate as the inputs. If you are honest about your current operation — not how you wish it ran, but how it actually runs this week — the output is diagnostic-grade. The financial cost calculator uses your own numbers. The pillar scores reflect your actual answers. It is a mirror, not a benchmark.
What happens after I run the audit? +
You receive a PDF report with your scores, priority sequence, and financial cost. If any pillar scores below 50%, you will see a fix action. You can act on those yourself using the individual tool for that pillar, or book a diagnostic call to discuss what the Installation would look like for your specific team.
The Installation
What does the 8-week Installation actually include? +
Layer 1: Hiring Architecture (judgment-based hiring profile, three-stage screening framework, interview templates) and Compensation Design (quality-based commission model, 90-day transition plan, quota calibration). Layer 2: All five pillars installed inside HubSpot — Targeting Engine, Gatekeeper filters, Consistency Layer scorecard, Diagnostic Cadence rhythm, and Signal Tower with all four signal workflows live and firing. Week 12 review session included.
What do we need to have in place before starting? +
HubSpot (or a CRM you are willing to migrate to HubSpot). At least 3 SDRs running outbound sequences. Leadership sign-off on the compensation transition. That is it. The Installation builds the rest from scratch.
How disruptive is the Installation to the existing team? +
Less disruptive than most teams expect. The system is installed alongside existing operations in weeks 1 to 4, then transitions in weeks 5 to 8. The compensation change is managed through the 90-day plan — no rep loses money during the transition. The Execution Scorecard is introduced as a coaching tool before it becomes a performance metric.
What happens after the 8 weeks? +
You own the system. All five pillars are running inside your HubSpot. The hiring profile and compensation model are documented. The diagnostic cadence is a habit. The week 12 review confirms everything is running correctly. After that, no ongoing retainer is required — though many teams choose to bring Wilberforce back for quarterly diagnostic reviews.
Can we start with just the Audit and decide on Installation later? +
Yes — and that is the recommended path. The Audit identifies exactly which pillars are broken and what they are costing you. That context makes the Installation proposal specific rather than generic. Most teams who run the Audit and see their financial cost number move forward with Installation within two weeks.
